zombie rivers

James Brasington, Waikato Regional Council Chair of River Science suggests that by confining rivers in Aotearoa thorugh stopbank impelmentation, we are creating 'zombie rivers'

As a result of stopbank implementation, water and sediment fluxes have increased the rate of incision beneath the stopbanks causing bank erosion. An implication of this sediment flux is sediment delivery to the coast, altering the morphodynamics of the Ōtaki River (Hooke and Bray, 1995). In relation to Knight (2018), our “zombified” rivers (Brasington, 2019) are creating legacies that will change how the river evolves in the future. It is the recognition that the Ōtaki River has the ability to manage and adjust itself (Jansson et al. 2005) that will guide effective future space to move initiatives, so that the river can maintain a natural variability between biotic and abiotic habitats (Blue et al., 2013).
